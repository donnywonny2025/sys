# Jeff Kerr — Writing & Communication Preferences
**Last Updated:** Feb 16, 2026

## TONE & VOICE
- Conversational and direct. Not corporate. Not stiff.
- Confident but not cocky. Shows conviction without sounding like a sales pitch.
- Should sound like a real person talking, not AI-generated copy.
- Prefers genuine motivation over performative enthusiasm.

## THINGS TO AVOID (AI Red Flags)
- **No em dashes everywhere** — the "Name — I'm" construction is a dead giveaway. Use periods.
- **No "I'll be straightforward" or "I'll cut to it"** type openers — these read as AI filler.
- **No inflated stats leading a sentence** — e.g., "one of my ads hit 10 million views" felt weird and braggy to Jeff, even though it's true. Weave achievements in naturally or leave them out.
- **No generic buzzwords** — keep it real, specific, grounded.
- Kill excessive em dashes throughout. Use commas, periods, normal punctuation.

## STRUCTURE PREFERENCES
- **Always introduce yourself.** Say hi, say your name. Don't jump straight into credentials.
- **Email body should be short** — a hook, not the full cover letter. 3-5 short paragraphs max.
- **Cover letter goes in the PDF attachment** — full detail lives there, not in the email.
- **Cover letter + resume in ONE PDF** — don't make them open two attachments.
- **Include the "if this position is filled" clause** — always leave the door open for other roles.

## CONTENT PREFERENCES
- **Lead with WHY you want the specific role/candidate** — not just "I have X years of experience."
- **Show urgency** — Jeff feels a real sense of urgency about the political moment. Reflect that.
- **Mention being a fast starter** — no ramp-up needed, can be editing within hours. This matters to him.
- **Use "Grand Rapids" not "Wayland"** — nobody knows Wayland.
- **Portfolio note:** "I usually try not to share too much political work publicly for obvious reasons" — this is the preferred phrasing. Natural, understood, not dramatic.
- **Don't say "private link" or "please keep it between us"** about the portfolio — just note that political work isn't easy to share publicly.
- **Mention GMMB + Obama 2008** — but frame it as experience, not as the centerpiece. It was a long time ago.
- **Mention working midterm cycles "off and on"** — honest framing, not overselling.

## PROCESS PREFERENCES
- **Open the email in Mail.app** — use mailto links to pre-populate compose windows.
- **From address:** colour8k@mac.com
- **Document everything** — keep drafts saved, track what was sent.
- **Research the candidate/company** — Jeff wants to know who he's pitching and show genuine knowledge.
- **File uploads on web forms** — STOP when you hit a file upload. Don't try to interact with the system file picker. Just tell Jeff which file to upload and where, and let him do it.
- **Cover letter addressing** — Use good judgment. If you know the person's name, use it. Otherwise "Hiring Team" or "To Whom It May Concern" depending on formality. Read the room on each one. Don't be generic.

## GENERAL
- Jeff values authenticity over polish.
- He'd rather cut something that sounds fake than keep something that sounds impressive.
- He knows his experience speaks for itself — doesn't need to oversell.
- Prefers action over deliberation — "just fix it and open mail again."
